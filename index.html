About

ENTERT TROUPE DANCERS.

Who are we & what we do

A group of associated persons working together to uplift the unprivileged children in the communities around Mirambi, Kigenya, Lwengwe and other places near Kyotera district. Entert troupe dancers uses art, music, dance and drama to instill life skills in the young generation to help them have a bright future. Entert troupe dancers, a troupe and a non-profitable organization offering charitable activities, we work a group to help the unprivileged people and carrying out vigorous trainings to perfect in dances, we also post all our activities both online and in field as a way of seeking for help and support for our communities. We participate in the development, well-being of the population and enhance education for cultural values through performing arts, music, dance and drama in a way to uplift the unprivileged or disadvantaged more so the children. We request for your support and guidance in a way to uplift the communities everywhere.

CHARITY’S AIM

A charity's aims have to fall into categories that the law says are charitable. These are things like preventing or relieving poverty, or advancing the arts, culture, heritage or science. Goats are important in resource-poor communities because they provide tangible benefits such as cash income from animal sales, meat for home consumption, manure, skins and fiber.

Banana value adding project

Banana Peeling, Drying and Packaging for Better Markets in East Africa.

Banana Peeling, Drying and Packaging is part of value addition that helps in food security enhancement in the region and at a Global scale given the wide spread importance of bananas as a food crop. Banana is a staple food for a large part of the population in the eastern African region. It is a perennial crop but with specific seasons of plenty and scarcity. In plenty seasons the prices of bananas get so low, yet during scarcity, the prices rise tremendously. This price fluctuation is caused by a number of factors ranging from seasonal variations to transport costs. The storage facilities are also poor or non-existing in urban centers where the matooke are finally sold. This often leads to ripening and decay of products before they are sold. There is also a problem of garbage accumulation and associated costs in town caused by banana peelings and stalks dumped in towns and city centers. There is limited knowledge on peeling matooke which discourages the young generation from consuming it. Our bio innovation is to peel fresh matooke, pack it, brand and distribute it to super markets for sale. Fresh matooke if peeled, packed and refrigerated can last one month. The unsold will be collected, dried, grounded and packed for distribution in supermarkets. This matooke flour can be consumed as food and porridge. The bio innovation is important because it is a solution to the high rate of perishability. It also reduces on the bulky nature, making it easy to transport and export. Similar efforts have been done in Uganda but on a small scope. Banana value addition project an idea implemented by ENTERT TROUPE DANCERS. The expected first results is increased numbers of matooke produced from an average of 10 to 20 bunches per month from each beneficiary farmer. A concern was raised to us regarding variation in supply and prices according to seasons. Perishability was cited as the main cause. There was another concern by consumers about the sap and peelings as an inconvenience that deters them from regularly consuming bananas. It is on this back ground that ENTERT TROUPE DANCERS a non-governmental organization in Uganda, would like to implement the Banana peeling and packaging project in the east African region.
